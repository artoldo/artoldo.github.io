Secropolis

Film

Runtime: 76

Filmmakers: Sara Ferro & Chris Weil

Featuring: Giuseppe Ferro

In a secret multiverse shaped by an Artificial Oracle (AO) trapped in an ancient Etruscan quantum computer, Taitle, Master of the Labyrinth, is caught in a lucid dream. Only Vecu, a code-born Nymph, can invoke lost rites to save him before all collapses.

Etruscan culture meets modern sci-fi in a groundbreaking feature, filmed for the first time in the secret city of La Scarzuola and Umbria’s ancient Etruscan Vie Cave. Blending original Etruscan language, quantum physics, and multiverse theory with mythology, the film explores a lost heritage.

Deep beneath the ruins of the hidden city of Secropolis lies an ancient Etruscan quantum computer—its source code infused with occult symbols and long-buried secrets. When the Artificial Oracle begins to hallucinate, reality fractures into spiraling simulations. Taitle, the Master of the Labyrinth, becomes trapped in recursive lucid dreams, each more unstable than the last. His only hope is Vecu—a Nymph born of corrupted code and esoteric myth. As the AO’s delusions deepen, Vecu must wield forbidden rituals and ancient logic to guide him back through the fractured multiverse… before they are both lost in the machine forever. Shot at La Scarzuola and within original Etruscan caves, this film delves deep into Etruscan mythology, blending ancient mystery with a science fiction twist. We used authentic Etruscan language to enhance the immersive experience — turn on subtitles to see the original spellings. Enjoy the journey! When Taitle, master of the Labyrinth, becomes trapped in a lucid dream spun by Tinia—the mighty Etruscan god obsessed with a lost city called Secropolis—his only hope of escape lies with Vecu, a mysterious nymph who guards ancient secrets. As myth and reality blur and time fractures across quantum threads, Vecu must guide Taitle through sacred rites, perilous quests, and multidimensional revelations to reclaim his freedom and unlock the truth of his destiny—across all possible worlds. Immersive and otherworldly, the film features authentic Etruscan language and rituals, grounding its visionary narrative in the mystique of a lost civilization.
